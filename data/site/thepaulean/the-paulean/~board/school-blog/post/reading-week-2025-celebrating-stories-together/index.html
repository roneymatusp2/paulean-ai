Reading Week 2025: Celebrating Books, Creativity, and Imagination
Apr 14 2025

Reading Week 2025 at St. Paul's was a joyful celebration of books, creativity, and imagination. Staff across the school planned a programme of activities to honour the power of stories and inspire a love for reading in every pupil.

Special Guests and Activities

Throughout the week, pupils engaged in a variety of workshops, competitions, and special events, including sessions with visiting authors. Across the school, pupils had the chance to meet British author Mark Grist, an award-winning poet and children’s storyteller, who led workshops and shared his passion for words with enthusiasm and humour.

Form 1 pupils were treated to a special talk with Brazilian author Noemi Jaffe, a writer and professor of Literature and Creative Writing.

In the Junior School, pupils enjoyed engaging storytelling sessions with César Obeid and Paula Mello. César, a “cordelista”, writer, and speaker, brought a unique and captivating approach to literature through stories told with strings and improvisation. Paula Mello delighted pupils with a warm and heartfelt story that encouraged them to reflect on their own love of reading.

Reading Week Traditions

Midweek, the entire school embraced a beloved tradition — Dress as Your Favourite Book Character Day. From solo costumes to group ensembles, pupils and staff showcased their creativity.

In addition, Senior School pupils took part in the annual door decorating competition, while in Junior School, lunchtime was filled with themed activities, including bookmark-making sessions.

Pupils also submitted original bookmark designs, which were then printed and shared during library sessions—a lovely keepsake from a week filled with stories.

To bring the week to a close, three of our prefects visited our youngest pupils in PP3 to read a story about kindness. This moment captured the essence of Reading Week—celebrating the joy of storytelling and bringing the school community closer together through books.
